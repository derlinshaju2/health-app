Understanding Nutrition Basics

🥗 Macronutrients Explained

💪
Proteins

Function: Building blocks for muscles, bones, and enzymes

Sources: Lean meats, fish, eggs, dairy, legumes, nuts

Daily Need: 0.8-1.2g per kg body weight

🍞
Carbohydrates

Function: Primary energy source for brain and body

Sources: Whole grains, fruits, vegetables, legumes

Daily Need: 45-65% of total calories

🥑
Fats

Function: Energy storage, hormone production, nutrient absorption

Sources: Avocados, nuts, olive oil, fatty fish

Daily Need: 20-35% of total calories

🌟 Micronutrients Essentials

Vitamins
  • Vitamin A: Vision, immune system, skin health
  • B Complex: Energy metabolism, brain function, red blood cells
  • Vitamin C: Collagen synthesis, immune support, antioxidant
  • Vitamin D: Bone health, immune function, mood regulation
  • Vitamin E: Cell protection, skin health, antioxidant
  • Vitamin K: Blood clotting, bone metabolism
Minerals
  • Calcium: Bones, teeth, muscle function, nerve signaling
  • Iron: Red blood cells, oxygen transport, energy metabolism
  • Magnesium: Muscle function, energy production, protein synthesis
  • Zinc: Immune function, wound healing, DNA synthesis
  • Potassium: Fluid balance, nerve signals, muscle contractions
  • Sodium: Fluid balance, nerve transmission, muscle contraction

Hydration & Beyond

💧 Importance of Hydration

  • Body Composition: 60% water, essential for all bodily functions
  • Brain Function: Even mild dehydration can impair cognitive performance
  • Digestion: Essential for nutrient absorption and waste removal
  • Temperature Regulation: Sweating and cooling mechanisms
  • Joint Health: Lubricates joints and protects cartilage

🎯 Daily Hydration Goals

  • General: 8 glasses (64 oz) daily as baseline
  • Exercise: Add 1-2 glasses for every hour of activity
  • Hot Weather: Increase intake to compensate for sweat loss
  • Pregnancy: 10+ glasses daily for fetal development
  • Signs of Adequate Hydration: Pale yellow urine, rarely thirsty

🍵 Healthy Beverage Choices

  • Water: Best choice - zero calories, pure hydration
  • Herbal Tea: Antioxidant-rich, no caffeine
  • Vegetable Juice: Nutrient-dense, watch sodium
  • Green Tea: Metabolism boost, antioxidant benefits
  • Limit: Sugary drinks, excessive caffeine, alcohol

Mindful Eating Practices

🎯 Core Principles

👂
Eat Slowly

Take 20+ minutes to finish meals. Your brain needs time to register fullness.

🍽️
Use Smaller Plates

Research shows people eat 30% less when using smaller plates and bowls.

🚫
No Distractions

Eat away from screens, TV, and phones. Focus on your food and company.

😋
Savor Each Bite

Put your fork down between bites. Notice flavors, textures, and aromas.

🛑 Stop When Satisfied

Hunger Scale: Eat when moderately hungry (3-4), stop when satisfied (6-7)

The 20-Minute Rule: Wait 20 minutes before getting seconds

Portion Awareness: Start with smaller portions, get more if still hungry

Emotional Eating: Ask if you're eating for hunger or emotions

🏷️
Understanding Food Labels

📊 Serving Size Matters

Check the serving size first! All other information is based on this amount. You might eat 2-3 servings without realizing it.

⚠️ Watch for Hidden Sugars

Look for these in ingredient lists: sucrose, high fructose corn syrup, dextrose, maltose, fruit juice concentrate. Limit foods with sugar in the first 3 ingredients.

🧈 Sodium Content

Daily value: 2,300mg for healthy adults, 1,500mg for those with high BP. Processed foods often contain excessive sodium.

🔍 Ingredient Order

Ingredients are listed by weight. If sugar or unhealthy fats appear in the first 3 ingredients, it's not a healthy choice.
