🏥 Common Chronic Diseases

❤️
Cardiovascular Disease

Leading cause of death globally. Includes heart attacks, strokes, and other circulatory conditions.

Risk Factors: High BP, cholesterol, smoking, diabetes, obesity
🩸
Type 2 Diabetes

Chronic condition affecting how body processes blood sugar. Can lead to serious complications if unmanaged.

Risk Factors: Obesity, inactivity, family history, age, ethnicity
🫁
Respiratory Diseases

COPD, asthma, and other lung conditions that affect breathing and overall oxygen intake.

Risk Factors: Smoking, pollution, occupational exposure, genetics
🧠
Neurological Disorders

Conditions affecting the brain and nervous system, including stroke risk and cognitive decline.

Risk Factors: Age, high BP, diabetes, smoking, genetics

🌟 Lifestyle Medicine Guidelines

🥗 Nutrition Guidelines
  • Plant-based focus: 5+ servings of fruits and vegetables daily
  • Whole grains: Choose brown rice, whole wheat, quinoa over refined
  • Healthy fats: Avocados, nuts, olive oil, fatty fish
  • Limited processed foods: Avoid added sugars, excessive sodium, trans fats
  • Hydration: 8 glasses of water daily, limit sugary drinks
🏃 Physical Activity
  • Aerobic exercise: 150 min moderate or 75 min vigorous per week
  • Strength training: 2+ sessions per week for all major muscle groups
  • Flexibility: Regular stretching or yoga to maintain mobility
  • Daily movement: Break up prolonged sitting every 30 minutes
  • Consistency: Find activities you enjoy for long-term adherence
😴 Sleep Optimization
  • Duration: 7-9 hours of quality sleep per night
  • Consistency: Regular sleep schedule, even on weekends
  • Environment: Cool, dark, quiet bedroom
  • Pre-sleep routine: Avoid screens 1 hour before bed
  • Limit stimulants: No caffeine after 2 PM, avoid alcohol before bed
🧘 Stress Management
  • Mindfulness: 10 minutes daily meditation or deep breathing
  • Social connection: Maintain strong relationships and support networks
  • Time in nature: Outdoor activities for mental health benefits
  • Hobbies: Engage in enjoyable activities regularly
  • Professional help: Seek therapy if stress becomes overwhelming

🚨 Warning Signs - When to See a Doctor

❤️ Heart Attack Warning Signs
  • Chest pain or discomfort
  • Shortness of breath
  • Pain or discomfort in arms, back, neck, jaw
  • Cold sweat, nausea, lightheadedness
🧠 Stroke Warning Signs (FAST)
  • Face drooping
  • Arm weakness
  • Speech difficulty
  • Time to call emergency
🩸 Diabetes Warning Signs
  • Excessive thirst and frequent urination
  • Unexplained weight loss
  • Blurry vision
  • Slow-healing sores or frequent infections
